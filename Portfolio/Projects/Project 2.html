Case Study 02

Responsive Website

The Product

Glampy allows the user to book glamping sites throughout Denmark, Sweden, and Norway. The typical user is 19-50 years old. As glamping provides the opportunity to camp in the comforts of your own home, the age range is fairly wide.

Project Duration

July 2023 - August 2023

The Problem

Available glamping sites have cluttered and outdated designs that are difficult to navigate or they do not go in-depth specifically for glamping

The Goal

Design a responsive website that satisfy user needs in terms of information and functionality about glamping specifically

My Role

UX Designer

My Responsibilities

Conduct interviews, paper and digital wireframing, low and high-fidelity prototyping, usability studies, designing and iterating on those designs

Understanding the user

I conducted user interviews in combination with a competitive audit which revealed the need for a more detailed glamp-site compared to what is available on existing booking sites. The few sites that does provide enough information is visually cluttered and difficult to navigate, which is why I decided to create a sitemap to visualize the user flow

1

Information

Current booking sites does not provide enough detail specifically about glamping

2

Experience

Current booking sites are outdated and difficult to navigate and does not provide an engaging browsing experience for users

User Journey Map

Mapping Jakob's user journey helped identify possible pain points and improvement opportunities

Sitemap

Confusing navigation was one of the user pain points, so I created a sitemap to make stragetic information architecture decisions. The structure I chose was to make it simple and easy, but with the ability to enter flows from different starting points

Starting the design

This time around I went directly to digital wireframes because the responsive nature of the webpage seemed easier for me to do digitally. In this phase I also created a low-fidelity prototype and conducted a usability study

Digital Wireframe Home Page

The structure of the home page allows for three different, but intuitive navigational elements that continues the main user flow

Digitial Wireframe Prototype

Usability Study

Using the low-fidelity prototype, I conducted a usability study that especially shed light on some issues regarding the booking process

1

Booking structure

Users found it confusing that personal information and card details were seperated between alternative login options

2

Booking dates

Users were missing booking dates on the payment screen

Refining the Design

In this phase, I created the mockups based on the previous user research feedback, made a high-fidelity prototype, and included accessibility considerations

One of the original user pain points were insecurity about booking glamp-sites with limited information. One of the ways I tried to address this was to add seasonal icons to convey which time of year the particular glamp-site is suited for

Making sure to inform the user whether the glamp-site is accessible with wheelchair

2

Used different heading with diferent sized text for clear visual hierarchy

3

Alt text is available for each page, as well as images

Takeaways

Impact

"I really appreciate the relevant information on each page, I'm not left with a feeling of tension because of something I was unsure of when I booked a glamp-site" (Participant 3)

What I learned

What I think something should look like and what the user actually needs are two different things. This perspective really came to light during this project. I also learned that the nitty-gritty things can have a big impact

Next Steps

1

Conduct more user research to determine if the app further decreases pain points

2

Determine if there are additional features that users would want
