Case Study 01

Mobile App

The Product

Evnt is a musician booking app that helps users quickly and easily find a musician for their upcoming event

Project Duration

June 2023 - July 2023

The Problem

Current musician booking sites does not let you book directly through the app. It usually requires sending an enquiry

The Goal

To make a design that fulfills users needs in terms of information, all while being able to book musician directly through the app

My Role

UX Designer

My Responsibilities

Conduct interviews, paper and digital wireframing, low and high-fidelity prototyping, usability studies, designing and iterating on those designs

Understanding the user

To empathize with users, I conducted interviews, created affinity diagrams, user journeys and
a competetive audit to fully understand what users actually need from the app.

1

Time

Many working adults are too busy spending time with event planners

2

Right Musician

Events such as weddings often have to be perfect for many people. It can be hard to find the right musician

3

Outdated Designs

Current booking sites are outdated and difficult to navigate

User Journey Map

Mapping Karen's user journey revealed how important it was to include relevant information throughout the app.

Starting the Design

In this phase I created paper wireframes, digital wireframes, low-fidelity prototype, and conducted a usability study.

Paper Wireframes

Paper wireframes saved me time iterating on the designs when coming up for ideas to adress pain points. For the home page, I tried to make it as easy as possible to find a musician.

Digital Wireframes

Moving into digital wireframes, I tried to make sure that the designs aligned with findings from user research.

Low-Fidelity Prototype

The low-fidelity prototype connected the primary user flow for booking a musician. This allowed for a usability study.

Usability Study: Findings

I conducted two rounds of usability studies. Findings from the first study helped me move from wireframe to mockups. The second study, using a high-fidelity prototype, helped me refine the mockups

Round 1 findings

1

Add a searchbar

2

Remove "when/where" bars

3

Make booking feel safe and direct

Round 2 findings

1

Include "view artist" button on artist cards

2

Make editing booking details more visible

3

Remove brand color on various elements for a simpler look

Refining the Design

In this phase I created the mockups based on the user research and made a high-fidelity prototype. I also added accessibility considerations.

Mockups

The designs allowed for some changes. After usability studies, I added a link on the artist cards as some users were confused about their clickability. Also changed chips content to only show event types. Genres can now be changed through filters.

The purpose of the app is to give users enough relevant information so that they feel safe booking the musician directly through the app. To adress this I included star ratings for all musicians as well as additional information such as comments and number of gigs under the 'About the Artist" section

Accessibility Considerations

1

Added alt text to images for screen readers

2

Made sure to include both text and video with sound for people with hearing or visual impairment

Takeaways

Impact

The app meets the user's desire for finding the right musician for their event in a quick and enjoyable way.

Quote from peer feedback:
"The app made it a whole lot easier to book a musician and I also felt it was trustworthy and safe during the booking process". (Participant 1)

What I learned

I learned the importance of research and getting feedback. This enhanced my design both in terms of functionality, but also visually.

Next Steps

1

Conduct more user research to determine if the app further decreases pain points

2

Determine if there are additional features that users would want
