Case Study 03

App & Website

The Product

Neighborfood allows users to list groceries and food they don't need on the app for people nearby to receive as opposed to throwing them out.

Project Duration

September 2023 - October 2023

The Problem

The challenge with this app is the motivation to get users to list groceris as well as recieving them. The app must encourage users to list groceries

The Goal

This app tries to solve social issues such as lack of food or economic instability in certain neighborhoods/areas as well as reducing food waste

My Role

UX Designer

My Responsibilities

Conduct interviews, paper and digital wireframing, low and high-fidelity prototyping, usability studies, designing and iterating on those designs

Understanding the user

In this project, interviews was the most valuable source of information to empathize with users. Since the app designs for social good, I needed to fully understand what challenges my potential users were going through

1

Price

Groceries are getting increasingly expensive

2

Health

Many people are forced to buy fewer groceries/ingredients for meals, leaving out many key groceries such a vegetables

Starting the design

The empathize phase made it clear that the app should be build around familiarity since this is an app that helps neighborhood/areas where people are likely to know each other. I tried to incorporate that in my wireframing. I also created a low-fidelity prototype and conducted a usability study

Digital Wireframe

To emphasize the familiarity perspective, I made the app profile based and included a messaging system. The cards on the home page are users and their available groceries.

Low-Fidelity Prototype

Usability Study: Parameters

Using the digital wireframe, I conducted the first of two usability studies

Study Type

Unmoderated usability study

Location

Denmark, remote

Participants

4 Participants

Length

20-40 Minutes

Usability Study: Findings

The usability study revealed a problem regarding one of the initial user pain points. Users wanted a way to search for specific food categories. There were also some confusion on the request groceries page.

1

Categories

Users want to easily find specific food categories

2

Available Groceries

Users were confused if they were requesting all groceries on a profile

Refining the Design

With the research and usability study findings in mind, I created mockups and a high fidelity prototype. I also included accessibility considerations

The usability study highlighted an issue regarding categories. To solve this, I created category chips on the home screen for users to click to show specific categories. This also reduces original pain point regarding lack of specific food types due to high prices.

I also made a small but important change to the text on the request button to clearly communicate that the user is only requesting some items if that is the case.

3

Takeaways

Impact

"I can really see this app making a difference for people in my area and I think everyone would and should be able to use it" (Participant 2)

What I learned

Designing an app for social good is eye opening in terms of empathizing with users. If you do it right, you can make a profound difference. Designing this app and website also taught me something about the theme and feel of a design, in this case the familiarity and down to earth perspective

Next Steps

Conduct more user research after going live to determine if the app further decreases pain points

2
